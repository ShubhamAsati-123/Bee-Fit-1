About

Diet refers to the types and amounts of food that a person consumes regularly. A healthy and balanced diet includes a variety of foods that provide essential nutrients, vitamins, and minerals needed for good health. This includes whole grains, fruits, vegetables, lean protein sources, and healthy fats.
A person's dietary needs can vary depending on factors such as age, gender, activity level, and overall health. Some people may also have specific dietary restrictions or preferences based on cultural, religious, or ethical beliefs.
Maintaining a healthy diet can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. A registered dietitian can provide personalized recommendations for a healthy and balanced diet.

TYPE OF DIET

Atkins Diet

A low-carbohydrate, high-fat diet designed to promote weight loss.

Mediterranean Diet

A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, seeds, and healthy fats such as olive oil. It is associated with a lower risk of heart disease, diabetes, and some types of cancer.

Vegan Diet

A diet that excludes all animal products, including meat, dairy, and eggs. It is associated with a lower risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, and some types of cancer, but requires careful planning to ensure adequate nutrient intake.

Vegetarian Diet

A diet that excludes meat but includes dairy and eggs. It is associated with a lower risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, and some types of cancer, but requires careful planning to ensure adequate nutrient intake.

DASH Diet

A diet that emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein sources, and low-fat dairy products, while limiting saturated and trans fats, salt, and added sugars. It is designed to lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease.
